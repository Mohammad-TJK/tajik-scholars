üìå What Makes Tajik Identity Distinct from Iranian Persian?

Though Tajiks and Iranian Persians share a common language (Persian/Farsi/Dari), they are distinct peoples with different histories, homelands, and national identities. Tajiks are the indigenous Iranian-speaking people of Central Asia ‚Äî specifically Khorasan, Transoxiana (modern Tajikistan/Uzbekistan), and Afghanistan. Iranian Persians descend from the people of the Iranian plateau (modern Iran).

The Tajik cultural sphere produced its own dynasties (Samanids, Saffarids, Tahirids), its own literary tradition centred in Bukhara and Samarkand, and its own scholars rooted in Central Asian cities. When Iranian nationalist historiography claims figures like Ibn Sina or Al-Khwarizmi as "Persian," they often erase this specifically Central Asian ‚Äî Tajik ‚Äî dimension of their identity.
